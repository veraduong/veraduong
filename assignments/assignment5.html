Challenge 5: Accessibility Concerns

For Part 2: I did not change / revise anything in this challenge as I received full points!

An accessibility issue is when there's a barrier that makes it difficult for a person with a disibilty to equally access, navigate, or engage with content on a website. An issue that could apply here on these HTML challenges is not having buttons with alt text. This can apply to forms or just general navigation on a page. For example, if there's a submit button that allows users to submit their name or search something in a search bar, putting in alt text with something spefic like "submit first name" will allow them to understand what is supposed to happen when the button is clicked and its purpose. Another possible accessibility concern relates to challenge 2 with tables. To make a table accessible it needs proper HTML markup that indicates header cells and data cells and defines these relationships. Many assistive technologies will use this information to provide context to users who can't rely on visuals. Relying on visual cues alone isn't sufficient and without proper markup people using screen readers will run into barriers. Lastly, relating to the chess board, unicode characters are inaccessible to screen reader users since they may be skipped over entirely or it may read something completely irrelevant which may make users guess what the intended message or visual was supposed to be.
